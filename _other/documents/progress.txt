1. Linear, max-anchored: 100 × ale[L1] / max(ale[L1])
Simplest, but most safeguards end up <10. Comparison with Risk Relevance (which is base-anchored around 80) will feel skewed.

2. Linear, share-anchored: 100 × ale[L1] / sum(ale[L1]) × scaling
Same problem — the share of a single safeguard out of 153 is tiny.

3. √-transform, max-anchored: 100 × √(ale[L1]) / √(max ale[L1])
Compresses the tail so mid-tier safeguards land in the 30–60 range. Reads similarly to Risk Relevance. This is what I'd pick. It preserves rank, the top safeguard is still 100, and the visual distribution looks like the Risk Relevance column.

4. Percentile rank: 100 × (rank / 153)
Perfectly even spread but loses magnitude information — a €500k safeguard and a €5k safeguard separated by one rank look the same. Not recommended.


Once both scores are 0–100, the interesting thing is the combination:

- Both high → top priority (financial pain + structurally relevant)
- Risk high, Financial low → governance/compliance value, low loss exposure (still do it — but maybe at lower IG)
- Risk low, Financial high → outsized loss leverage in a control your profile doesn't normally flag (often downtime/recovery controls)
- Both low → defer or inactivate

You could show the two as separate badges and let the user eyeball it, or show a combined score (risk + financial) / 2 and let them expand to see the split. I'd start with two side-by-side badges since they encode different things and combining them too early hides exactly the disagreement that's most useful.

TL;DR recommendation                                         

financialRelevance(s) = round(100 × √(ale_L1(s)) / √(max_s ale_L1(s)))

Compute once in the per-safeguard spectrum (cheap), pass through safeguardBadges (or a parallel map) into GapCatalog, render next to the existing Risk Relevance badge. Two scores, two colors, same scale.

        A) Non-linear EBI recovery friction:

              Replaced the linear formula downtimeDays × 0.5 with the quadratic 0.15 × downtimeDays²:
